4. The article “Why Accessibility Is at the Heart of Data Visualization” presents ways to make data visualization more accessible to people with visual disabilities. My dashboard does not implement any of these features so it would be difficult to access for people with visual disabilities. Furthermore, there are titles in each one of the charts in my dashboard but I do not present a summary of the data or of what the chart is representing. This makes the dashboard difficult to access for people not familiar with the data or what I’m trying to show with the charts. Lastly, I did not take into account for the pie chart, the different types of color blindness mentioned in the second article. Therefore, this chart might be difficult to access for individuals that suffer from these disorders. To make my data more accessible to a larger audience, I could have implemented features to aid people with visual disabilities and color blindness disorders, as well as including explanations of what each chart is representing.

5. I did not include any tools for people with visual disabilities because of the difficulty that adding these features involved. Furthermore, I did not take into account the different color blindness disorders because I could not find a scale of colors that were different enough for the pie chart and also took this into account. Thus, I chose different colors, some of which are not the best choice for people with color blindness disorders. Lastly, I did not implement a summary of the data and the chart because the audience that is going to look at my dashboard is familiar with the data and task of each chart.
